Technical Glossary

Unit 1: The Technological Process.

Technology

A set of knowledge and techniques that, when applied logically and orderly, allow human beings to modify their environment to satisfy their needs.

Technological Process

An orderly method for solving problems, consisting of a sequence of phases to design, build, and evaluate solutions to stated needs.

Sustainable Development

Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs, considering the environmental impact of a product's life cycle.

Identify the Problem

The first phase of the technological process, where a need or problem is identified and formulated, specifying the requirements that the solution must meet.

Information Search

A phase that consists of gathering data from different sources (books, internet, etc.) about similar problems and their solutions to generate creative and viable ideas.

Design

The phase in which the best solution is chosen and represented through sketches, drafts, and plans. The materials and tools to be used are selected.

Construction

The manufacturing phase of the designed object, following the plans and work plan. It is essential to respect safety regulations throughout the process.

Evaluation

The final phase of the process, where it is verified whether the constructed object works correctly and meets the initial need. It allows for identifying flaws and proposing improvements.

Workshop

The place where the construction phase of technological projects is carried out. It must be organized into specific zones for work and for storing tools and materials.

Tools

Instruments used to perform a job, allowing for the transformation of materials. They must be handled correctly and in accordance with safety rules.

Safety

A set of rules and precautions that must be followed in the workshop to prevent accidents. This includes using personal protective equipment and handling tools and machines properly.

Emergency Plan

A protocol of action that must be known in case of an accident or risky situation in the workshop. It includes the location of fire extinguishers and emergency exits.

Tidy and Clean

A fundamental principle for safety and efficiency in the workshop. It involves keeping the workspace and tools in their proper place, and cleaning up material residues upon completion.
